Telugu is a Dravidian language primarily spoken in the Indian states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, where it is also the official language. It is spoken by approximately 96 million people (2022), making it the most widely spoken member of the Dravidian language family.

Telugu has an unbroken, prolific, and diverse literary tradition of over a thousand years. It accepts foreign words with comfort and ease, and Sanskrit has heavily influenced Telugu literature. The language has a unique script, consisting of 56 letters, written from left to right.
